Horchata

Description

Horchata is a traditional Mexican drink made up of white rice soaked in water, it’s flavored with cinnamon and its sweetened with granulated sugar. Everything is minced up together in a blender and later strained to remove solids. Some versions are dairy free while others contain milk and some versions contain nuts or added flavor like vanilla or coconut.

Ingridients:

  • Long grain white rice
  • Sliced almonds
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Cold water
  • Granulated sugar(1/2 cup)
  • Vanilla
  • Whole milk

Instructions:

  1. Blend rice mixture: add rice, almonds and cinnamon sticks to a high speed blender. Blend about 30 – 60 seconds on high speed or until finely pulverized.
  2. Sweeten: pour in 2 cups water, sugar and vanilla. Blend an additional 30 seconds.
  3. Add remaining liquid: if your blender can fit the additional liquid pour in remaining 2 cups water, and milk, otherwise pour into a large enough container to fit along with water and milk.
  4. soak: cover and chill in refrigerator 8 – 12 hours.
  5. solids: strain mixture through a fine mesh sieve into a pitcher.
  6. chilled: serve with ice if desired, garnishing each serving with ground cinnamon or cinnamon sticks.
